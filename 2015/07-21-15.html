Community Leadership Summit (CLS)

The weekend before OSCON, there's another conference going on in the same conference center, called Community Leadership Summit. It first started in 2009 by Jono Bacon 

(author of "The Art of Community"), and has been running every year since then with the help of volunteers and nice sponsors. It's free for attendees-- consider joining next year if you head to OSCON.

 

The event itself has a few main sessions in the morning and then it's an unconference during the rest of the day, with round-table discussions and presentations on topics such as Scaling your Community, Identifying Top Influencers, Podcasting, and Hacking Communities with Behavioral Economics. It was an awesome event and EMC {code} will try to include a few of the lessons learned from CLS in the near future, so keep an eye out on the Slack team and our blog! 

 

Thanks @jonasrosland for the writeup!

Welcome three new {code} team members! 

 

The EMC {code} team has been fortunate to gain three great new team members over the past few months. Without further ado, please meet: 

 

Drew Smith

Based in the bay area, Drew came to EMC through the CloudScaling acquisition and was a natural fit for the EMC {code} team. A self-proclaimed Linux nerd since '95, Drew has been involved in emergent/disruptive technology for most of his career and specializes in 12-factor design, big cloud orchestration, metrics and monitoring. You can find him on GitHub and Twitter.

 

Steve Wong

Based in the SoCal area, Steve joined EMC {code} from EMC's Core Technologies Division, where he designed and developed backup and replication products. Steve worked on a cloud scale backup product - the backup service within vCloud Air.  Steve spends nights and weekend time learning about DevOps tools, going to tech meetups, and mentoring a local high school robotics team. You can find Steve @cantbewong (best username ever) on Twitter and GitHub. 

 

Andrew Kutz

Austin, Texas-based Andrew Kutz joins EMC {code} from the Solutions and Partner engineering team at EMC. Andrew was responsible for not only architecting/creating and developing products but establishing DevOps flows and tools within his teams. Among other things, he excels in going deep with languages, software architectures, and control plane aspects of storage platforms. Andrew will be focused on Docker and storage, when he's not spending time with his family and five (!) dachshunds. You can find Andrew on Github as akutz!
