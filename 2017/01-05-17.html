As a team we have created, published and participated in many different Go-based projects over the past  two years, learning best practices from each other and the community along the way. So we thought that this would be  something you may appreciate...

Little did we know that one of our team members, Vladimir Vivien, would have some fabulous news to share when we got back from our time off, namely publishing a book! Vlad has been writing a book called "Learning Go Programming" over the past year and a half, crafting a great introduction to Go for newcomers.

In his own words:
"I have attempted to write the book I would have liked to have read when I was starting out with Go. It distills the language specs, the documentations, the blogs, the videos, slides, and my own experiences of writing Go into content that carefully provides the right amount of depth and insights to help you understand the language and its design."

Here's more on why Vlad wrote a book on Go (including where to find it).

2017 Container Trends: Winter is Coming

Now that 2016 is over, we look to what 2017 brings and of course, one can't help but ask: have the battle lines been drawn in the container space? According to Joshua Bernstein (VP of Technology, Dell EMC),  2017 appears to be the start of what may determine the rest of the container industry for the foreseeable future.  He writes that this coming year will reveal the next steps in the revenue models for container companies, product features and  other business practices. Find out more about Josh's predictions on the blog and don't forget to leave a comment or share your feedback.
