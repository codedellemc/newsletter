{code} Community

The {code} Community is growing like wildfire and as we gain more members we are also taking the next step in its evolution.

Today we are launching a new series of bi-monthly public webinars with a focus on interesting open source projects. We want to make sure the {code} Community stays informed on new and exciting topics. We are happy to announce that we also have a new landing page where you can find out more about future and past webinars.

Our first public webinar will be on April 6th at 1PM EST with none other than Matthew De Lio, Product Manager at Google for Kubernetes and Google Container Engine, giving us a Kubernetes overview and an update on what's new in the 1.6 release.

The {code} team has been hard at work making sure Kubernetes and Dell EMC ScaleIO work together, and we are very happy to see that functionality be a part of the 1.6 release. You can read more about our work with Kubernetes and ScaleIO by reading our blog post by Vladimir Vivien.

Please join us on April 6th by heading over to our webinar site and RSVP!

{code} Org.

It is with great excitement that we announce Dell Technologies has joined the Cloud Native Computing Foundation as a Platinum Sponsor. As an open source group focused on software-based infrastructure the {code} team has always been an advocate of the cloud native approach. Our participation in the Cloud Native Computing Foundation further signals our commitment to ensure the growth of a healthy and interoperable cloud native ecosystem that provides choice and confidence for customers optimizing their applications for cloud environments.

We are thrilled and honored to join an already impressive group of companies, community members, and individuals - especially our fellow platinum members Cisco, CoreOS, Docker, Fujitsu, Google, Huawei, IBM, Intel, Joyent, Mesosphere, Red Hat, Samsung SDS and Supernap.

For more information about this announcement, please see  our blog and press release.
