One month til DevOps @ EMC World!  

 

The odds are in your favor if you're headed to Las Vegas for EMC World. Or if you just need a good excuse to come to EMC World-or heck, to come to Las Vegas for a while. Here's what's in store:

 

DevOps @ EMC World:

[Open to the Public] On Sunday, May 3rd from 2-7pm, EMC {code} is hosting a completely free event for EMC World attendees, local community members, and anyone who is interested in learning more about what DevOps means, what tools and processes make it successful, and how leading companies have successfully transformed. There will be speakers from Dropbox, LinkedIn, Moogsoft, EMC, Intel and more. We know it can be a long journey to Vegas, so we're offering luggage storage and cold beers, sodas, and snacks on us. REGISTER HERE

  

DevOps Breakout Sessions:

[Open to EMC World pass holders] Throughout the week, there will be breakout sessions on DevOps and Next-Gen applications, highlights include: "Containers vs. VMs," "The DevOps Toolkit," "DevOps is a Re-Org, How to Accelerate the Change" and a handful of others. Check them out and hope to see you there.

 

Open @ EMC World

[Open to EMC World pass holders] EMC {code} will be part of the Open @ EMC World area along with some neat partners: Mirantis, HortonWorks, Puppet, Splunk, and Cloudera, to name a few. Come by to talk to some smart people from these companies, say hi to EMC {code} or just unwind with a really good cup of coffee, comfortable seating, and plenty of space to charge your devices.

 

If you can't make it this year...

What happens in Vegas, will probably end up on GitHub. So if you're not able to make the event, we'll make sure you can consume as much of the content as possible from the comfort of your own office, home, mom's basement, man cave, woman cave, etc.

 

Let us know if you're coming! Send a tweet to @EMCcode and we'll  see you there!

Meet #DevHigh5 Winner, Evan Battle!

Introducing #DevHigh5 Winner, Evan Battle

 

One of our first #DevHigh5s, Evan Battle wanted to simplify snapshots with XtremIO. 

 

Chad Sakac says it best in his original blog post on EMC {code}': "Evan Battle is a great Sr. SE in the US who wanted to build an automated way of managing the awesome new XtremIO snapshot functions in the 3.x codebase, and didn't wait to ask :-)

 

His code example, XtremIOSnap creates and maintains XtremIO snapshots using the REST API. XtremIOSnap also provides a working example of interacting with the XtremIO API."

 

Thanks for your contributions, Evan! You can find Evan on GitHub and Twitter.
