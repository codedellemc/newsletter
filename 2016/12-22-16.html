{code} Community

The {code} Community was quite busy in 2016.
 
With 20+ new open source projects created, published and promoted in the last year, we are humbled and thankful to be involved with so many passionate people who are interested and willing to spend their free time creating and innovating in a way that benefits everyone. We've seen projects range from Golang-integrations to lab setups to new and interesting monitoring methods. A big thank you to our DevHigh5 members! 

This year we were able to meet more of you in person. At some events we hosted {code} Assemblies, where we got together a group of very smart and interesting individuals who wanted to spend time with one another talking about how they blend tech, life and open source.  We are looking forward to hosting more of these in 2017, so if you're at an event with us please find us and ask where and when the {code} Assembly is meeting. 
 
Earlier this month we launched the {code} Catalyst program, which is very special to us. Comprised of open source focused individuals from many different companies and organizations, our hope is to build a great place for knowledge sharing to lead and advance emerging technology within our own and other's organizations. A big thank you to the first {code} Catalysts; we'll be spending a lot more time with you in 2017. 

It's never too late to join the {code} Community. 

{code} Org.

As 2016 comes to an end, we are joining others and pausing to reflect on how the year went, who we met and where the next year may lead. For us, we are grateful that we met you. We are jazzed with how explosive this year was for the {code} Team. Only 2.5 years into existence and we are in awe of what we have accomplished. These are a few of our favorite things:
  • Met over 10,000 people at various conferences
  • Major contributor to Mesos 1.0
  • REX-Ray ships with DC/OS from Mesosphere
  • Tripled the {code} Community 
  • Released over 20 updates to various projects
  • Attended and staffed over 25 conferences and meetups
  • Hosted three Mars Challenge Hackathons
  • Produced a Nationally televised special for 1.25M viewers
These are just a few highlights of what this year has been for us! We are going to recharge next week in order to take on 2017.
