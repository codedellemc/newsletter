Tools of the Trade 
If we're lifting the veil on our code, why not the tools that help our team run as smoothly as possible? EMC {code} developer advocate @jonasrosland is the pied piper of new SaaS tools and has brought in some great applications that make our lives easier every day.

Here are the tools that power our team communciation. We'll talk more about the developer toolkit in a future issue.

Communication:
  • Slack- viva la chatroom! Slack has reduced our emails significantly and made communication across distances and timezones easy and mobile. People seem to agree.
  • ConstantContact- an all-in one contact list (helps us with event registrations and communications-- all in one platform
  • Wordpress- The best in the blogosphere. It helps that we have someone with mad design skills (@kendrickcoleman)

Collaboration:
  • Trello- the digital version of sticky notes/whiteboards. We use Trello for planning CFPs, newsletter topics, and tracking status updates on our #DevHigh5 gifts.
  • Quip- Like Google Docs, but better. We take meeting notes in the "open" on Quip.
  • KnackHQ- We track community projects on Knack
  • GitHub- well, yeah.
Let us know if you have any must-have communication tools for developer communities. Drop us a line @EMCcode

Introducing Qurt & #DevHigh5 Creator, Daniel Moran

 

Submitted by EMC'er Daniel Moran, the Quarterly Utility Reports Tool (QURT) is an add-on utility for EMC's RecoverPoint for VMs product, that is available as open source software. QURT provides an easy way to monitor the usage of one or more RecoverPoint for VM system s. It is intended for users who wish to generate a quarterly report of replicated Virtual Machines. 

 

A standalone web-container exposing REST services and AngularJS client and sampling RecoverPoint systems for virtual machines (VM) data. 

 

Check out QURT on GitHub and make sure to follow Daniel for some more cool projects!
