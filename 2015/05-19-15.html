HELLO WORLD

It's been a couple weeks now, and it's just now dawning on us that EMC open sources technology. The CoprHD project  brought that to light at EMC World. EMC had been doing it for over 18 months with OpenStack contributions, and EMC {code} has been doing it for about 9 months, but there's something about it being in a major press release that makes it real. And as EMC President Jeremy Burton said, "It's Not Your Father's EMC" anymore. Everything we do is focused on open source, so it great to see the market understand that this is long-term EMC strategy. 

 

We hope you'll enjoy this newsletter. We'll do our best to distill, aggregate and share news related to the {code} community and industry happenings.We'll provide the content, but we need your feedback. You can always reach us at emccode-newsletter@emc.com

OpenStack Vancouver

 

EMC has a big presence at Openstack Vancouver this week, and EMC {code} was lucky enough to be invited along. If you're at #OSV, check out the EMC events happening throughout the week. On the first day of the summit, the EMC "State of the Stack" panel was standing room only; make sure to save your seat for the repeat session later this week.
EMC {code} Demo Station - 2nd floor in the expo center 

 

The EMC {code} team has a demo and game pod outside the Design and Ops summit on Level 2-- stop by to meet the team there, learn about EMC {code} and the projects we're working on, CoprHD (open source version of the ViPR Controller, announced last week), and play ViPR Strike Pacman (!!)

 

If you can, stick around on Thursday afternoon. EMC {code} Developer Advocate @Ragss is delivering a hands-on workshop on Cloudfoundry on OpenStack. See you there!

Cloudfoundry Summit 2015

 

 

A handful of {code} team members attended the CF Summit in California last week. The summit has grown in size and scope, especially from the 2014 summit, where the attendees were mostly vendors.

 

Based on information freely discussed at the Cloud Foundry Summit 2015 - 60% "vendors" and 40% "customers" - it would not be difficult to begin with the premise that the Cloud Foundry ecosystem is finding a healthy balance between creating technology achievements and solving market problems. 


Read the full recap blog from @bgracely here, and check out some of the recorded sessions on YouTube if you weren't at the summit last week.
