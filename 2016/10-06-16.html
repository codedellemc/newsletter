{code} Community

As is tradition, at large open source conferences the {code} team arranges community dinners. This past Tuesday at LinuxCon/ContainerCon Berlin we invited community members to join us for a night filled with german food, drinks and great conversation. We went to the oldest biergarten in Berlin, the Prater Gaststätte, where the discussions ranged from containers to broken database schemas to finding love.
The {code} team appreciates our community full of easy-going and fun members with diverse interests. You make our events so much fun.

Again, thank you all for joining us this past Tuesday and we hope to see you at another event soon!

Do you want to be a part of these community dinners? Reach out to Jonas Rosland on our {code} Community Slack!
{code} Org.

The keynotes at LinuxCon/ContainerCon EU this week have not disappointed. Chris Schlaeger spoke about 10 lessons learned in 10 years of EC2, project InfraKit open sourced live in traditional keynote style from Solomon Hykes (Founder of Docker) and the CTO of Suse, Thomas Di Giacomo presented an homage to Mr. Robot as "We are LSociety" encouraging us to maintain open source values. 

During the closing keynote session (the latest trend to wrap up a conference) we heard about the continued push of community around open source and how tech companies have begun to influence open source software. One of the main things tech companies are doing is providing paid positions to work in open source like our team here at {code}. It's incredible to see the influence that companies now provide to the open source community. 

Head to the Linux Foundation YouTube channel for the recorded keynotes next week.
