Including everyone's taste buds in your community
 
Some of the most popular ways of connecting with others in the community are to attend conferences, meetups and hackathons. Usually, these events have a vendor or organization that sponsors food and drinks, and some of the most popular ways of ingesting nutrients at these events are through pizza and beer. You grab a slice, pop open your bottle and mingle. 

We are not saying to take away the pizza and beer. 

But what if someone doesn't want or can't eat pizza? What if you or some of your attendees don't drink beer or alcohol? Then the options are usually a salad and a coke. We don't know about you, but this doesn't exactly sound fun or inclusive. In fact, we think - as a community we can do better.
 
Having alternatives to pizza doesn't have to be daunting. If you have a sponsor or a vendor, ask your contact about vegetarian/vegan options, gluten-free alternatives, peanut-free food or other accommodations. Many restaurants now offer a wide range of healthy and inclusive options. That make staying away from pizza all the more interesting and convenient.

When it comes to drinks we are truly lucky because there are so many great options. It's almost impossible to choose between great choices like sparkling water that tastes amazing, great coffee in bottles and a large assortment of juices with no sugar added.
 
When volunteering or working at events, it really pays off to spends a little more time thinking about what kind of sustenance the attendees would like to have. Try it out, we're pretty sure you'll like the new options!

FlexREX adds Storage Options to Kubernetes

In this blog,  Steve Wong lays out the in-tree and out-of-tree process for Volume Plugins in Kubernetes. Through this breakdown he briefly explains the benefits and downsides. He then introduces our latest contribution called FlexREX. 

FlexREX is a plugin that enables Kubernetes to talk to REX-Ray, and then REX-Ray in itself uses libStorage to talk to storage underneath.  This is in addition to direct support of Docker and Mesos, which makes REX-Ray a single tool that supports many container platforms with one common tool and experience.

Read the full details in the blog here.

New release announcement: REX-Ray v0.7.0 now offers Ceph support!
